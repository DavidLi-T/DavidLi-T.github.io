Header 3

David Li is a postdoctoral fellow working with Dr. Rebecca Wells at the University of Pennsylvania. His research interests include mechanobiology and biocompatible fabrication, with a current focus on the relationship between histological features and stiffness in hepatic steatosis and fibrosis. He has received the 2023 Postdoctoral Research Fellowship Award from the American Liver Foundation and an NRSA T32 Postdoctoral Fellowship Award for his recent work.

Working with Dr. Wells, he developed a technique to associate tissue mechanics with histology at the mesoscale, and has published in FASEB Bioadvances how liver fat accumulation causes softening on the macro and meso scales. In his doctoral work with Dr. Yu-li Wang at Carnegie Mellon University, he discovered that cells coordinate collective movement through Wnt signaling, published in PNAS. While training with Dr. Joyce Wong at Boston University, he studied biomimetic biofabrication of silk, published in Biofabrication.
